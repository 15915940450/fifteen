農曆 （中國傳統曆法）

陰陽曆,lunisolar calendar,Chinese Calendar,agricultural calendar

24 Solar Terms

二十四節氣，2016年，聯合國教科文組織保護非物質文化遺產委員會，正式將中國二十四節氣列入聯合國教科文組織人類非物質文化遺產代表作名錄。

  • The Beginning of Spring (立春)： Spring begins
  • Rain Water (雨水)： It begins to rain
  • The Waking of Insects (惊蛰)： Hibernating animals come to sense
  • The Spring Equinox (春分)： Day and night are equally long
  • Pure Brightness (清明)： It is warm and bright
  • Grain Rain (谷雨)： Rainfall is helpful to grain

  • The Beginning of Summer (立夏)： Summer begins
  • Lesser Fullness of Grain (小满)： Kernels plump
  • Grain in Beard (芒种)： Wheat grows ripe
  • The Summer Solstice (夏至)： It has the longest daytime and the shortest night of the year
  • Lesser Heat (小暑)： Torridity comes
  • Greater Heat (大暑)： The hottest moment of a year

  • The Beginning of Autumn (立秋)： Autumn begins
  • The End of Heat (处暑)： Heat hides
  • White Dew (白露)： Dew curdles
  • The Autumn Equinox (秋分)： The mid of autumn
  • Cold Dew (寒露)： Dew is very cold
  • Frost's Descent (霜降)： Frost descends

  • The Beginning of Winter (立冬)： Winter begins
  • Lesser Snow (小雪)： it begins to snow
  • Greater Snow (大雪)： It snows heavily
  • The Winter Solstice (冬至)： The shortest daytime and the longest night of a year
  • Lesser Cold (小寒)： It is rather cold(小寒勝大寒，常見不稀罕。)
  • Greater Cold (大寒)： The coldest moment of a year
